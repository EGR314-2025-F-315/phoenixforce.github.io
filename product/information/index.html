Product Information

Device Overview, Audience, & Metrics

High-level information for reviewers: what we learned on our visit, the device goal, who it serves, and the engineering metrics we will measure.

Overview - What We Learned

Where hotspots emerge

  • South-facing slopes; wind corridors/canyons; heavy fuel accumulations
  • Human ignition zones: powerlines/transformers, trails/campgrounds, equipment staging areas

Site & policy constraints

  • Zoning/WUI rules, right-of-way, access roads, water sources
  • Mounting height & line-of-sight, comms coverage, power availability
  • Environmental sealing (IP rating) and local noise limits for buzzers

Engineering metrics to track

  • Time-to-detect; false-alarm rate
  • Localization/perimeter error
  • Message-delivery reliability & latency
  • Uptime/runtime under battery power
Sensor Pros & Cons (Quick Take)
Sensor Strengths (+) Limitations (−)
Thermal IR Sees heat through smoke Sun-heated rocks/metal can fool; higher power draw
Smoke / PM Catches smoldering early Dust/humidity outdoors → false positives
UV/IR Flame Fastest for open flame detection Needs line-of-sight; solar interference filters required
Gas (CO/VOCs) Good near ignition sources Signal dilutes quickly in open air
RGB + CV Perimeter & classification capability Needs compute/lighting; potential privacy concerns
Goal of the Device

Primary objective

Prevent & detect early; and if ignition occurs, guide responders with fast, clear, geo-tagged information.

Key capabilities

  • Local sensing with clear, glove-friendly on-device alerts (screen + buttons)
  • Perimeter estimation and geo-tagged reports to a base map/tablet
  • Internet-optional comms (mesh/peer link) with acks/retries and link-loss UX
Measurable Targets & Definition of Success

Targets (course-fit)

  • Detect stimulus in ≤1–2 minutes (lab surrogates)
  • Device↔base alert/telemetry latency ≤1 s (lab)
  • Perimeter/localization error ≤25–50 m (field surrogate)
  • ≥24–48 h battery runtime; weather-resistant enclosure plan
  • ≥99% message delivery (n ≥ 100)

Definition of success

  • Repeatable demo hitting targets
  • Clean documentation: build steps, wiring/schematic, BOM, short demo video
  • Portfolio-ready package others can reproduce
Audience & Use Cases

Primary users

  • Fire lookouts & watch programs; park rangers & land managers
  • First responders: wildland crews, incident command, utility fire-prevention teams

Use cases

  • Fixed lookout kit (tower/ridge) and rapid-deploy mobile unit (vehicle/tripod)
  • Automatic alerts to a base tablet with map overlays & acknowledge/assign flow
  • Training/demo mode for drills and community education

Value proposition & adoption

  • Faster, clearer situational awareness with fewer false alarms
  • Operates where public networks are weak; ≤60-second learnability
  • Creates logs/metrics for after-action reviews
  • Pilot in a high-risk corridor → tune thresholds → scale to multi-site deployments
Safety & Ethics
  • No open-flame tests; use safe surrogates for heat/smoke signals
  • Respect privacy when using cameras; limit retention and focus on hazard imagery
  • Document misuse considerations and provide clear warnings to users
