Summary

Project snapshot

Mission

TerraGuard rover and screen host their own Wi‑Fi AP, stream metrics and MJPEG video, and alert through LEDs and speaker when gas readings look risky. Built to work offline and remain glove-friendly.

  • Two boards: rover (sensing and compute) and screen (HMI).
  • HTTP GET polling to rover-hosted AP at 192.168.4.1.
  • LEDs and speaker on both boards for clear alerts.

Highlights

  • Stable AP link with continuous metrics and MJPEG stream.
  • Gas classifications drive LEDs and audio on both boards.
  • Power rails verified; GPS and drive tested on final PCB.
  • Poster, UI mockups, and comm sequence captured below.

Power

Power budget

Single 7.4 V pack feeds 5 V and 3.3 V rails with at least 25 percent headroom.

Rail Load Current (mA) Regulator Headroom
5 V L298N motor driver 800 L7805CV (1500 mA) About 500 mA remaining
3.3 V ESP32-WROOM, BME688, MQ-2 logic, camera, GPS, programmer About 567 LM3940 (1000 mA) About 433 mA remaining

Battery: 7.4 V 10,000 mAh pack at roughly 454 mA estimated draw gives about 22 hours theoretical before converter losses. Power budget guided trace sizing and kept LEDs on 5 V to isolate the MCU rail.
